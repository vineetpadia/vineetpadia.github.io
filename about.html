About

An engineer based in Boston.

I graduated from the University of Maryland with a major in Mechanical Engineering (2017). I also participated in undergraduate research with Dr. Siddartha Das in the SMIELS lab. I was also active members ASME, Terrapin Hackers, UMD Hyperloop groups on campus.

I have been working for Festo as a product development engineer, focusing in microfluidics and laboratory automation. Over the last two years, I helped create a new team and facility for rapid development cycles at the company. I spent most of my time designing and developing lab automation components and fluid dispensing systems. My interests range from microfluidics, soft robotics, MEMS, and precision mechanical design.

In my free time I enjoy exploring the city, visiting muesums, taking various MOOCs via various online platforms, and hacking together control systems with Arduino/Raspi and 3D printers.
