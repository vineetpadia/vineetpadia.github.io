Introduction

  • The Hyperloop is a new form of transportation which involves a levitating pod moving through a low pressure tube to allow for convenient, efficient, and safe transonic travel.

  • Our pod operates using entirely passive magnetic levitation, braking, and control in order to achieve speed in excess of 200 mph!

Problem

Develop a levitation system that can support the weight of the pod, allow high speed travel and minimize the response to vibration.

System Description

Passive magnetic levitation reduces friction with the track and avoiding common issues with wheels at high speeds. While magnetic drag has a large peak at low velocities prior to levitation, its lift to drag ratio only improves with speed.

Design

  • The levitation array was designed to be able to levitate the pod after being pushed up to 5-6m/s.
  • The suspension was designed hold the weight of the frame, and to absorb any the loads during an acceleration or brake of 1g.
  • Each levitation array required a minimum harmonic frequency of 120 Hz, to safely avoid the frequency of gaps in the track at speed and to allow for safer handling.
